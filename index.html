Global Climate Summit 2025: Leaders Promise Action

World leaders gathered this week in Geneva for the 2025 Global Climate Summit, amid mounting pressure from scientists and environmental activists. Delegates from over 190 countries pledged to cut carbon emissions and increase investment in renewable energy projects. Experts highlighted that this summit could mark a turning point in international climate cooperation, stressing the importance of accountability and clear timelines.

Discussions covered energy policy, carbon taxation, and emerging green technologies. Youth activists attended rallies outside the venue, demanding immediate action to prevent catastrophic climate effects. The conference concluded with a joint statement emphasizing collaboration and innovation, but critics argue the commitments may fall short without binding regulations.
